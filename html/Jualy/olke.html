Geographical location

Zhualy district is located in the upper and middle reaches of Teris (Asy) river, most of its territory is occupied by the Zhualy mountain plateau (height 900 m in the center, 1200 m at the edges). It is surrounded by the Karatau ridge in the north and northeast, Boraldai mountain in the west and southwest, and Talas Alatau in the south. In the north of the district is Bilikol pothole (absolute height 450 m). Coal and construction materials are found in mineral resources.

Social forms

There are 62 educational institutions under the Department of Education in the district: 46 general education schools (30 secondary, 7 basic, 9 elementary) 13 kindergartens 1 art school 1 clay school 1 correspondence school
In total, 1502 teachers are providing education and training to students in district schools. Among them: 1295 are specialists with higher education, 207 with special secondary education. Among them - 194 of the highest category, 429 of the first category, 628 of the second category.
There are 2 sports schools for children and teenagers in the district. Sports circles were opened for boxing, free Kazakh, ju-do wrestling, togyzkumalak, football, volleyball, basketball, and stone lifting.
There are big and small football, basketball and volleyball fields in the territory of Momyshuly village. 1 stadium with 1500 seats has been launched. Also, there are a total of 24 sports halls, 85 sports fields, 10 shooting ranges and 1 billiard hall in the settlements of the district.
There are 46 cultural institutions belonging to Zhualy district. They: 1 regional center of folk creativity and cultural recreation 8 rural culture houses, 11 rural clubs 1 district, 1 rural children's library 24 rural libraries
Also, there are 3 collectives with the title "People's", they are "Kyzgaldak" song and dance ensemble, "Zhualy", "Mynbulak" folk theaters. Since 1924, the district newspaper "Zhana Omir" has been published.
In the district, there are religious places of two main denominations: Islam and Christianity. In total, there are 25 mosques, 1 church and 3 religious houses. According to today's data, the total number of visitors to religious places is 589 people. Kazakh, Slavic, Uzbek, Azeri and Chechen - a total of 5 cultural and national centers work in the district. All of them were founded in 1993 and serve to promote the traditions, culture and language of their nation.
Today, the district has a central hospital with 174 beds, a tuberculosis hospital with 25 beds, a clinic for the reception of 250 people, 10 medical clinics providing primary health care and 38 medical centers.
